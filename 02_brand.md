## §1 — Brand Architecture

### The system
| Name | What it is | Context |
|---|---|---|
| **iN** | The tool, the habit, the act | App, newsletter, the verb |
| **begiN** | The community layer | Mighty Networks, the movement |
| **begiNners** | The member noun | Lives inside "beginners" |
| **Smith-Karlson LLC** | The parent entity | Legal, investor, Apple Developer |

### Canonical spelling
- `iN` — lowercase i, uppercase N. No period. This is the official name.
- `begiN` — lowercase b-e-g-i, uppercase N. No period. The lowercase "beg" matters: it preserves the relationship to iN.
- `begiNners` — the N carries the brand inside the word.
- Do NOT write: `BegiN` / `BEGIN` / `begIn` / `IN` (without lowercase i)

### The period rule
The period is an **editorial and typographic treatment** — not the legal name.

Use `iN.` in:
- Deck headers and slide titles
- Editorial lockups and pull quotes
- Display headlines with intentional emphasis

Do NOT use in: legal docs, email copy, body text, domain names, social handles.

`notice. capture. begiN.` — this is a brand line, not a sentence. The periods are intentional punctuation marks, not trailing punctuation.

### The italic N rule
Italic N = display treatment only (wordmark, headlines, deck). In prose and body copy, the capital N alone carries it. Never force italic in running text.

## §2 — The Mark

**iN compound glyph:**
- Left stroke = lowercase `i`
- Four-point glint = the dot of the `i` (awareness / presence)
- Right stroke = uppercase `N` with downward arrow embedded (inward movement / capture)
- The N is the **hero glyph**. Dense with meaning. Never simplify it.
- The symbol rewards attention over time. Meaning unfolds gradually.
- Simple at first glance. Intelligent upon inspection.

**Stroke weight:** Slightly heavier than early explorations. Not chunky, not playful. Enough heft to survive small sizes, feel premium, and maintain app-icon presence. Weight should feel deliberate and stable.

**The glint:** Diamond-like, not swooping. Sharp but not spiky. Intentional, not magical. Luminous, not decorative. Should feel like awareness appearing — NOT a sparkle sticker, AI twinkle, fantasy star, or sci-fi flare.

**The arrow:** Embedded through negative space. Taller than wide arrowhead. Sharp enough for clarity, softened enough to stay human. Should feel discovered within the form, not pasted on. Readable at small sizes.

**Proportions:** Vertical stance, tall silhouette. Iconic at 16px and elegant at poster scale. Avoid squat proportions, excessive softness, or exaggerated roundness.

|---|---|---|
| Filled | `resources/in-deck/icon_filled.gif` | Primary iN mark |
| Outline | `resources/in-deck/icon_outline.gif` | Watermark / begiN context |

**Visual authority reference:** `resources/in-deck/Apple_Brand_Board_Final.png`

**begiN mark (B):** Reserved. When designed, the B should be:
- Simpler and more open than the N
- More communal, slightly softer, more human
- NOT as symbolically dense as the N
- The asymmetry is intentional: awareness precedes participation

**Rule:** The N is the hero glyph. The B should not try to match its intelligence.

## §3 — Color System

### Emotional architecture
| Color | Role | Emotional function |
|---|---|---|
| Forest black | Background / depth | The unknown. Space. Interiority. |
| Citrus-hi | iN accent | Signal. Awareness. Electricity. Consciousness. |
| Orange/Amber | begiN accent | Ignition. Human warmth. Invitation. Beginning. |
| Warm white | Body text | Legibility. Calm. |

**The citrus thinks. The amber moves.** These are not interchangeable. Do NOT use orange for iN (the tool) or citrus for begiN (the community). The distinction carries meaning.

**Display exception:** In the tagline `notice · capture · begiN`, both the i and N of "iN" may be rendered in citrus. This shows the iN mark living inside the word begiN — the brand architecture made visible.

### Dark palette (native — app, deck, primary brand)
| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Forest black | `#0b1810` | Primary background |
| Forest | `#1e3a1f` | Secondary panels |
| Mid green | `#3d5f21` | Tertiary / muted |
| Citrus | `#a6e300` | Brand accent (secondary) |
| Citrus-hi | `#d4ff59` | iN accent (primary electric) |
| Orange | `#EF9300` | begiN accent |
| Warm white | `#f5f4ee` | Body text |

**Citrus on black** is the native system. This is where `#d4ff59` lives. It was designed for dark backgrounds.

### Support palette (functional neutrals — not emotional brand colors)
| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Deep Surface | `#111A14` | Cards, panels, elevated modules, overlays |
| Graphite Moss | `#1E2A22` | Dividers, inactive states, subtle containers |
| Soft Ash | `#A7AEA7` | Secondary text, metadata, timestamps |
| Warm White | `#F5F5F0` | Light backgrounds, editorial layouts, breathing room |

Never pure white.

### Dark theme hierarchy
| Layer | Hex |
|---|---|
| Primary background | `#0B1810` |
| Elevated surfaces | `#111A14` |
| Structural accents | `#1E2A22` |
| Primary text | `#F5F5F0` |
| Secondary text | `#A7AEA7` |
| Signal text | `#D4FF59` |
| Activation text | `#EF9300` |

### Light palette (Substack, email, editorial) — locked May 27, 2026
`#d4ff59` blooms as highlighter on white. `#7EA800` collapses into olive/eco. The locked light accent is a middle-frequency editorial signal that preserves the citrus character without neon bloom.

| Layer | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Primary background | `#F5F5F0` | Warm white — never pure white |
| Secondary surface | `#ECEDE7` | Cards, panels |
| Structural line | `#D7DBD2` | Dividers, borders |
| Primary text | `#111A14` | Body copy |
| Secondary text | `#516055` | Metadata, captions |
| **Signal accent** | **`#B4D61A`** | **Citrus for light mode — links, buttons, highlights** |
| Amber accent | `#C46E00` | begiN / community elements on light backgrounds |

**`#B4D61A`** is "phosphor ink not glowing light" — still unmistakably iN, avoids forest/olive associations, holds up in typography.

Reserve `#D4FF59` for: dark mode, logo, hero graphics, active signal states only.

### Never
- Purple (drifts feminine startup — not this)
- Highlighter neon (the citrus should feel *luminous*, not gaming)
- Oversaturated gaming greens
- Soft wellness pastels
- Background gradients or gradient washes on color fields (reads as generic startup)

**Note:** A luminous gradient *within the iN mark itself* (bright citrus at top, slightly deeper at base) is acceptable if it reads as the mark being lit — alive, signal emergence. This is different from background gradients. Amy's call per mark version.

## §4 — Typography System

| Role | Font | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Brand / headlines / UI | Söhne | Headings, navigation, key statements, hero typography, brand lockups |
| Body / interface | Inter | Body copy, app UI, Substack content, forms, metadata |
| System / machine / labels | DM Mono | Prompts, timestamps, tags, metadata, terminal moments |

**Söhne** — intelligence, restraint, precision, calm confidence. Architectural and premium without hype. (Paid — Klim Type Foundry.)

**Inter** — clarity, usability, readability. Dominates day-to-day product and reading.

**DM Mono** — system language, machine layer, prompts. Used sparingly. Never in reader-facing copy. The Brigettes feel it without seeing it.

**Hierarchy rule:** Söhne = intelligence and signal. Mono = machine underneath. Inter = the human bridge. All three coexist; none dominates.

**The tension is the point.** Soft outer form / sharp internal intent. Do NOT resolve it into a single coherent feel. The electricity comes from the unresolved tension.

## §6b — Photography & Motion Direction

### Photography
Avoid: forests, leaves, eco imagery, mindfulness visuals, wellness photography, spa aesthetics.

Use: atmosphere, signal, low-light interfaces, phosphor glow, precision surfaces, cinematic contrast, reflections, soft industrial textures, illuminated edges, subtle grids, sensor aesthetics.

Imagery should feel: **technologically human.**

### Motion
Restrained. Slow. Intentional. Calm. Precise.

Avoid: bouncy UI, exaggerated easing, flashy transitions, excessive particles.

Motion should feel: **like signal emergence.**

## §7 — What This Brand Is Not

The deck explicitly avoids and the brand should never drift toward:
- Purple (associated with feminine startup aesthetic — not this)
- Wellness clichés (soft gradients, rounded everything, "healing" language)
- Generic AI aesthetics (blue glows, circuit patterns, robot imagery)
- Cyberpunk tropes (neon on black ≠ forest black + citrus)
- Productivity maximalism ("optimize your life")
- Authority and arrival energy ("I figured it out, let me teach you")
