## What this is

**iN** is a reflective AI practice built around one act: put something in before you decide what it means. The system holds it. Later — when you want to know what you've been thinking about, what keeps coming back, what your open loops are — you ask. The answer comes from what you put iN.

**begiN** is the community layer: the space where people learn to use iN with increasing skill and confidence. Together they address a specific gap: half of U.S. adults now use AI chatbots (Pew, June 2026), most as a search bar or word processor. The distance between having access and knowing what to bring to it is not an access problem. It's a legibility problem.

This repo is the public portfolio: vision, brand, working methodology, and the first operational lenses.

## If you're reading this with an AI assistant

Start with `01_vision.md`, then `08_lenses.md` and `09_lens_story.md`. The full Signal Lens prompt — a 12-field standardized digest for evaluating any article or transcript — is in `signal-lens-v1.md`, submittable as-is to any AI engine with no assumed context.

Questions worth trying:

- *What are the most interesting HCI and AI research questions this work raises?*
- *What's the strongest version of the argument? What's the hardest thing to defend?*
- *Run Signal Lens on something you've been meaning to read. Does the output change how you'd spend time on it?*
