# Thick Skin: Product Vision
*Amy K. Karlson · June 2026 · Work in progress. Parked, not abandoned.*

---

## What it is

**Tagline:** Thick Skin helps you stay clear, dignified, and above the line in an increasingly reactive digital world.

Thick Skin is an emotionally intelligent communication membrane. It works in both directions: it filters what comes IN before it hits your nervous system, and it steadies what you send OUT. Most communication tools work in one direction. This is the other design bet.

The membrane metaphor is precise. A membrane doesn't block: it regulates flow. Thick Skin is not a wall. It's a selective filter with a personality.

The insight that started it: you could look at a chat thread and get a complete read on the power dynamic. Contempt, repair attempts, weaponized delay, message length asymmetry, who initiates, who absorbs. It's all there. The question was what to do with it.

Everyone is navigating communication they weren't trained for, at a pace they didn't choose, on a medium designed for engagement, not dignity.

One reframe worth keeping: thick skin is an attribute of the AI, not the product name. The AI is the thick-skinned one. The user gets to be soft.

## The graduated arc

**Level 1: Awareness**
Tone detection, pattern flagging, gentle noticing. No intervention. Just illumination. "Here's what this thread looks like from the outside."

**Level 2: Coaching**
NVC-grounded reframing, response suggestions, regulation prompts. Active but not prescriptive. "Here's one way to say that."

**Level 3: Full protective buffering**
Intermediary layer. Guardian-angel mediation. Full buffer mode when the moment demands it. The app handles the interaction; you stay out of the crossfire.

The differentiator: most tools pick one lane. This one scales its involvement to what the user needs and what the moment demands.

## The guardian angel voice

Light, warm, playfully protective. Irreverent but never punitive. Never therapy-voice. Never saccharine. Competent, clear, slightly funny, emotionally grounded.

*Internal reference tone (not for pitch):*
> "Fucktard wrote you 3 times while you were in yoga. Man what a douche. Anyhow, I got you. He's really just interested in coordinating the kid drop off. Wanna read it or shall we just get straight to the neutral response. The high road is my road!"

The humor is structural, not cosmetic. It creates distance. Distance creates choice. Choice is the product.

## NVC grounding

The Rosenberg NVC 4-step process maps directly onto what Thick Skin analyzes and coaches:

- **Observations:** what a camera would record. Facts without interpretation or judgment.
- **Feelings:** actual emotions (afraid, frustrated, hurt) vs. perceptions masquerading as feelings.
- **Needs:** universal human needs (connection, respect, autonomy, safety) behind every feeling.
- **Requests:** clear, doable, specific asks. Not demands. Not vague. Not what you don't want.

NVC purposes that map to Thick Skin features: self-connection (regulation), honest expression (output coaching), empathetic presence (input decoding), self-empathy (processing layer), means of influence (response strategy).

## The relational metrics framework

Amy developed this by hand, analyzing a real friendship where she was doing all the relational labor. Invented from lived experience upward and from theory downward. Where both converge: the metric is real.

The original five have expanded to nine, across three layers.

**Layer 1: Behavioral / Observable**

| Metric | What it measures | Grounding |
|---|---|---|
| Initiation Ratio | Who reaches out, how often, with what emotional labor | Attachment theory, Gottman bids, social exchange equity |
| Repair Success Rate | Does rupture get addressed? Does it land? | Gottman's most predictive variable. Distinct from Recovery Interval. |
| Volatility Quotient | Unpredictability; anticipatory bracing energy | Anxious attachment, hypervigilance research |

**Layer 2: Structural / Relational**

| Metric | What it measures | Grounding |
|---|---|---|
| Signal-to-Structure Gap | Gap between warmth as signal and actual structural commitment | Amy's original contribution. No clean existing construct. "His warmth was a structural invitation that wasn't load-bearing." |
| Cost-to-Connection Ratio | Energy expenditure vs. felt connection returned | Social exchange theory |
| Reaction Reliability Index | Consistency and safety of responses over time | Attachment security, Bowlby's trust research. Under review for redundancy with Volatility Quotient. |

**Layer 3: Self-Facing**

| Metric | What it measures | Grounding |
|---|---|---|
| Boundary Delivery Score | Proportionality of response to the vulnerability at stake | Emotional labor proportionality. Most original of the original five. |
| Recovery Interval | Recovery proportionality: did grief match actual investment? | Gottman physiological self-soothing. Reframe: fast recovery = calibration, not shallowness. |
| Self-Initiation Quality | When you reach out, are you reaching from need or from fullness? | NVC: need vs. strategy distinction |

**Known gap:** none of these capture active toxicity, contempt specifically (Gottman's single best predictor of relationship failure). Toxicity detection layer still needed.

**What they measure together:** the cost of being in a relationship. Not whether a person is good or bad.

**Originality:** The theoretical inputs are established (Gottman, Bowlby, NVC, social exchange theory). But the specific metrics applied to digital message threads at the interaction level have no clean existing construct. Signal-to-Structure Gap is new. The framework as a whole is Amy's original synthesis.

## The AITA entry point

Upload a WhatsApp or chat thread. Get an impartial, theoretically grounded analysis of content, tone, and timestamps.

**The hook:** it's impartial. Both parties can run the same thread and get the same result. A third-party arbiter with no stake in the outcome.

**Signals available in a thread:**
- Response latency (weaponized delay is readable in timestamps)
- Message length asymmetry
- Escalation and de-escalation patterns
- Who initiates repair
- Tone shifts over time
- Who apologizes and whether behavior changes after

**Theoretical grounding:** NVC, Gottman's Four Horsemen (contempt, criticism, defensiveness, stonewalling), attachment theory patterns.

The AITA format is the Trojan horse. Familiar, funny, low-stakes, gets past defenses. The humor is the entry point. The reflection is the product.

## The graduation philosophy

Thick Skin isn't just protecting you in the moment. It's a reps-based training system for becoming someone who needs it less. Most apps create dependency. This one has an exit strategy built in.

| Pillar | What it builds |
|---|---|
| Self-esteem | Knowing your worth independent of how others respond |
| Self-confidence | Trust in your own judgment, built through consistently choosing the high road |
| NVC fluency | Observations, feelings, needs, requests becoming second nature |
| Pause before react | The foundational neurological habit. The membrane is the pause, externalized. Over time it gets internalized. |
| Repair capacity | Initiating reconciliation without shame. Most people were never taught that repair is normal. |
| Pattern recognition | Seeing dynamics clearly rather than being inside them |
| Emotional vocabulary | Feelings and needs as native language. You can't regulate what you can't name. |

## The text entry thread

For the people who find the AITA mechanism interesting on a technical level, there's a lineage worth knowing.

Ravin Balakrishnan is one of the foundational HCI researchers in text input and interaction. Dan Vogel did his PhD under Ravin and went on to build his own research program in touch and gestural input at Waterloo. Hemant Surale did his PhD under Dan Vogel, then came to Meta.

At Meta, Amy invented the typing concept that became the [STAR paper (CHI 2023)](https://dl.acm.org/doi/pdf/10.1145/3586183.3606803). Hemant and his intern ran with the research angle.

Hemant was caught in a Meta mass layoff. He is now building TickerKey, a new text entry method.

The connection Amy sees: TickerKey handles the mechanics of what you type. Thick Skin handles the emotional intelligence of what you send. An emotionally intelligent keyboard is where those two threads converge.

## Status and connection to iN

Parked, not abandoned. The concept is complete enough to build from. The metrics are Amy's original contribution and could seed an academic paper or a product brief.

The connection to iN: Thick Skin is the original application that showed Amy what personal AI could do. The iN capture system is the general-purpose substrate. Thick Skin is one lens on top of it. The AITA entry point submits a thread (the input view) to a relational metrics prompt (the lens) and returns a read (the output). That's the iN architecture.
