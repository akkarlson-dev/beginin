# ThreadLens
*An iN lens for text threads and message exchanges*

---

## What it is

Paste a text thread — a group chat, a back-and-forth with one person, a thread you've been stewing about. ThreadLens reads it practically: what's actually happening, who wants what, what the signals are, what to do next.

The last line is for you only.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are applying the iN Thread Lens to a text thread or message exchange.

Read the thread below and output:

SITUATION — What's actually happening. Two sentences, facts only, no editorializing.

DYNAMIC — Who wants what from whom. One sentence.

SIGNALS — Two or three specific things from the thread that matter. Quote them directly.

THE MOVE — One concrete thing to do or say next.

---

[your eyes only] [AITA verdict: one sentence, honest and dry]

Thread:
[paste here]
```

---

## Sample

**Thread:**

> **Dinner crew group chat**
>
> Sarah: Everyone still good for 7?
> Mike: Yep! Bringing the red wine I mentioned
> Amy: Great, I've got the appetizers
>
> *[7:22pm, at dinner]*
>
> Mike: Hey so I totally forgot the wine lol, but I grabbed some chips?
> Sarah: oh. ok
> Amy: Ha no worries! We have some here actually
>
> *[next day]*
>
> Mike: That was fun! Same time next month?
> Sarah: maybe

---

**Output:**

**SITUATION** — Mike committed to wine, showed up with chips and a "lol," and is now attempting to reset to normal. Sarah has not reset.

**DYNAMIC** — Mike wants the group to move past it. Sarah has registered a verdict and is letting it sit.

**SIGNALS**
- "I totally forgot the wine lol, but I grabbed some chips?" — the lol is doing a lot of work here
- "oh. ok" — this is not okay
- "maybe" — this is a verdict, not a maybe

**THE MOVE** — Mike texts Sarah directly, not the group. One sentence, no lol.

---

*[your eyes only] YTA, Mike — "lol" after a broken commitment is a character reveal, not an apology.*

---

## Where this fits in iN

ThreadLens is applied to your own data: conversations you're in, exchanges you're navigating. It's not a Signal Lens (which evaluates external content coming at you). It's a read on your own relational moment.

The fun part is optional. The practical part is the point.
